These are not hypothetical. They have been sequenced, dated, and mapped across populations:SLC24A5 A111T — Skin LighteningThe SLC24A5 gene encodes a sodium/calcium exchanger protein located in melanosomes — the organelles where melanin is synthesized. The A111T variant (alanine replaced by threonine at position 111) impairs the protein’s function, reducing melanin synthesis significantly. This single amino acid change accounts for approximately 25–38% of the skin color difference between MC1R-functional and MC1R-variant populations — making it one of the largest-effect pigmentation genes in the human genome. The derived A111T allele is nearly fixed in European populations (95–100% frequency), found at high frequencies in Middle Eastern and South Asian populations, and virtually absent (&lt;5%) in sub-Saharan populations. This allele shows the strongest positive selective sweep detected in the human genome. Estimated origin: 20,000–30,000 years ago.OCA2 / HERC2 (rs12913832) — Blue EyesOCA2 (Oculocutaneous Albinism Type 2) regulates melanin production in the iris. The HERC2 gene contains a regulatory region that controls OCA2 expression. A specific variant in HERC2 (rs12913832) reduces OCA2 expression, resulting in reduced melanin in the iris and producing blue eyes. Genetic analysis demonstrates this mutation arose in a single individual near the Black Sea region approximately 6,000–10,000 years ago. Every blue-eyed individual alive today carries this same mutation, confirming a single origin and recent spread.
